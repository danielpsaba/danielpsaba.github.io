About

I am a master's student and administrative research associate at a university in Washington, DC, completing my degree this spring. My work sits at the crossroads of cognitive science, clinical psychology, and artificial intelligence — with a focus on how AI systems can be built to be trustworthy, interpretable, and aligned with human values.

I collaborate with Dr. Hanseok Ko at the Catholic University of America's Multimodal AI Laboratory and with researchers at Microsoft on applied AI safety questions. I am currently exploring PhD opportunities in computational cognitive science and AI safety.

Before pivoting to research, I pursued clinical psychology and developed a deep interest in how mental models and social cognition can inform safer machine learning systems.

Research

Multimodal AI for Public Speaking Analysis

CUA Multimodal AI Lab · Dr. Hanseok Ko

Developing a pipeline to analyze video of deacons delivering sermons, using multimodal AI tools to evaluate non-verbal communication cues — including tone, cadence, gaze, and confidence — with the goal of providing structured coaching feedback at scale.

AI Safety & Human Psychology

Microsoft Research · Taylor Black

Investigating how insights from human psychology — particularly social cognition and trust formation — can inform the design of AI systems that are safer and more interpretable in high-stakes decision environments.

Cognitive Development & Mental Representation

Graduate Research

Exploring how infants and children construct internal models of the world, with particular attention to Theory of Mind development, sibling dynamics, and the role of active participation versus passive exposure in learning.
